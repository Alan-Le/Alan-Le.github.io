Life Story:

As a 6 year old, I began to realize what being a good student meant. Having a good eduacation meant that I would live a life that I would want to have. At 7 years old I didn't live a fun year. For the most part, I learned ahead of my second grade classmates to be prepared for third. The next year kept doing the same as I had. Preparing and earning the good grades that I deserved. At nine, I started to realize how boring shopping could be, because my mom always forced me on her 2 hour trip to South Coast just to either go to Macy's or Victoria's Secret. When I grew a year older I began to mature more and actually grow way taller. At this age I reflected back at the time when my sister told me I would never be taller than her. I laughed and saw how short she was while she tried to chug gallons of milk hoping to become taller. At 11, I was in my last year of elementary. It became one of the most memorable years as we celebrated our last day. It was sad and happy as I was ready for middle school. Through my last two years I struggled for the first year but soon adapted to the new enviroment and teachers. Obviously for my 8th grade year I only was afraid of high school. Now I am here, inches away from becoming a freshman.
